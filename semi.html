Description

Penaeus semisulcatus is a widespread penaeid shrimp inhabiting shallow coastal waters of the Indo-West Pacific, including the Palk Bay region. It is recognized by its greenish body coloration, faint banding on the abdomen, and a moderately long rostrum. The species supports small-scale and commercial fisheries in northern Sri Lanka, particularly in trawl and stake-net catches.
